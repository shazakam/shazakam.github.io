The Intelligent Investor By Benjamin Graham

This book provides a very broad and robust philosophy to investing. Although most of th discussion is based around US Exchanges (and taxes, documents etc.) The fundamental statistical practices and psychological lessons can be applied to any investment decision. This revised edition comes with more modern day examples such as the dotcom bubble which was a pleasant surprise. The language is dry and to the point. The jargon occasionally confusing and requiring independent research to fully udnerstand the concepts presented. Tables of data and statistics which can bore. Yet this does not mean it ought not to be read. I recommend this particularly to individuals interested in Finance but also to those (who may have zero interest in finance) are interested in securing their financial security and future.

The quote to sum the book up entirely would be, "This too, shall pass away."

Rating: 8.5/10
