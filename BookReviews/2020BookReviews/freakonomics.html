Freakonomics by Stephen D Levitt

This book is very neatly summarised with the following quote: "If morality is the way we would like the world to work, economics represents how it actually works". Levitt poses a series of incredbily bizarre questions such as "What do Sumo wrestlers and teachers have in common?" or "What makes a perfect parent?" With the help of incomprehensible amounts of data he attempts to answer each question in an engaging manner with no need to delve into formulas but instead, by explaining the overall economic and moral explanation as to why the answer is so. It is also a very funny read.

Rating: 10/10
