The Da Vinci Code by Dan Brown

The Da Vinci Code is one of my absolute favourites and Dan Brown is one of my all time favourite authors. Brown is a master at blurring the line between fact and fiction which not only makes the book seem rich with interesting facts but at the same time makes you wonder 'Is this actually true?'. The Da Vinci Code follows the Harvard professor of history of art and symbology, Robert Langdon, cryptographer, Sophie Neveu, and the puzzle left behind by the beloved head of the Louvre, Jacques Sauniére after his gruesome murder at the hands of a cloaked albino.
What makes Browns writing so thrilling is his ability to present a mystery, giivng all the clues and then slowly perform an autopsy on what is in front of you with each new layer revealing another code ready to crack. The Da Vinci code is heavily focused on religious symbolism specifically between Christian and Pagan symbols. The novel explores various aspects of Western art, Christian and Pagan history, often touching on lesser known and potentially macabre thematics in the mentioned topics.
His wriitng is fast paced and quick witted. A potential side effect of this novel is a sudden urge to explore the entirety of the Louvre, a sudden interest in Christian and Pagan history and finally, an interest in Cryptography.

Rating: 4/4 Sénéchaux
