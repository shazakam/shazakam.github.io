Dante's The Divine Comedy:Purgatory translated by Mark Musa

Purgatory is a wonderful second entry into the Divine Comedy yet is outshone by Inferno. The beautiful cantos, brilliantly written, bring forth a sense of wonder and also importantly hope (compared to Inferno which brought forth a sense of abysmal despair and never ending suffering). The structure of Purgatory is interesting in itself. The Piligrim, before ascending to Heaven, must first purge himself of the Seven Deadly sin while at the same time embracing their respective opposite Cardinal Virtue. One each "terrace" he witnesses the punishment souls must endure before they can proceed further, however, he also sees/hear/experiences events in history of people who are the pinnacles of the sin being purger and the individuals who embody the opposing Cardinal Virtue.

Rating: 7/10
