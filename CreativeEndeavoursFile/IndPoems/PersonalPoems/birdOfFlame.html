Commentary

Sometimes you see the people you care most about go through disaster after disaster. However, we can help by providing a little spark. Pain and hardship are incredbily adept at transforming and strengthening character, but at times it may seem overwhelming. This is when it is important to have a jovial and smiling friend come in and with a little pat and a chat to lighten the mood and cause a flame to burn. It is then the friend's turn to sit back and simply admire and watch the transformation occur before their eyes.

My Darling Dear, My Bird of Flame, My Shooting Star

Troubled times will come,
Turmoil in darkness you run,
Hear my voice in the night wind,
Know I am not your fiend.

Hear me whisper “Burn”

Feel your wing grow frail,
Your sight turns pale,
Feel your muscles grow weak,
Shed tears, as you cannot speak.

Look to the quiet tree,
See up high, it is me,
A Nightingale in my turn,
Hear me sing those sweet words, it is time to Burn.

In a black abyss you hide,
No one but yourself at your side,
Lost in the chamber of your mind,
And gone is the time.

A man walks in rugged, old and grey,
He has put a smile on his day,
He takes out a mystical mythical urn,
And says “It’s been so long dear; it is time to burn”

He lies next to you and takes out his steel and flint,
Hitting them together causing sparks and tints,
Catching your wing, a flame is born,
You do not resist; you are too worn.

Flames doth rise ever higher,
Eyes close for the last time with desire,
Let you fly again, amongst the clouds,
As ethereal smoke quiet or loud.

Day breaks, the smoke has cleared,
It is not as the old man feared,
Clearing the ashes with his hands so sore,
He found you, more beautiful, than ever before.

Remade, Reborn and Rebirthed,
Made anew in a warm hearth,
Feel your strength, spread your wings and fly,
Tear a streak across the night sky.

Upon a mountaintop I will sit,
To marvel at celestial orbs in voids lit,
To see if I can spot you, near or far,
My darling dear, My bird of flame, My shooting star.
