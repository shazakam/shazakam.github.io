Commentary

My two week school trip to South Africa and Mozambique is one of my fondest memories. Spending a week in a bush camp in a a sweaty, smelly and mosquito ridden barrack surrounded by nature and cut off from the net was so much fun. I even got to pet a beautiful cheetah called N'Tombe (She was abandoned by her mother and hence, was taken in by the rangers and was used to being around people). We then spent a week in Bobole, Mozambique building a class room for the local primary school. Although labouring under the scorching african sun was demanding, at the end of the day you would feel as if you had accomplished somethiing. You would also become so grateful of what you have in comparison to those who have so little, yet still manage to smile.
