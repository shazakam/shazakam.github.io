Commentary

Who doesn't love turning 18? I had a wonderful dinner with my friends and I was brimming with excitement. I was excited to become an adult, to inch a bit closer to complete independence, the night before I had asked someone out for coffee on the coming Monday (who would later become my first love) and was very excited for that, and finally I was excited to soon finish highschool. Overall, it was just a period filled with energy and joy.

18th Birthday

It's the little things that make life matter,
Stopping the turbulent waves of any heartbreak shatter,
Perhaps a sailor on deadly seas will enjoy the perilous breeze in the company of fellow friends,
Who, even as the ship is going down will stay till the end of all ends.

I am now lucky man instead of a lucky child,
Although my companions may be wild,
Every single one has a trait I admire,
Perhaps my love for them is never ending, much like the eternal fire.

Melodic tunes colour the night sky,
As they all dance by.

A night of toasts,
A night of boasts,
But most importantly a night for the deadly sea breeze amongst my friends.
